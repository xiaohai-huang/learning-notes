Definition: the exponent or power to which a [base](011-exponents.md) must be raised to yield a given number. Logarithms are the inverse of exponents.

If $n = \log_a x$ then $a^n = x$, so $\log_a x$ tells what exponent is needed to make x from a.

## Laws of Logarithms

- $\log_a {1} = 0$
- $\log_a {a} = 1$
- $\log_a {(x \times y)} = \log_a {x} + \log_a {y}$
- $\log_a {x^y} = y\log_a {x}$
- $\log_a {\frac{1}{y}} = -\log_a {y}$
- $\log_a {\frac{x}{y}} = \log_a {x} - \log_a {y}$
- $\log_b {x} = (\log_b {a}) \times \log_a {x}$
